First, I encourage educators — especially those who teach in high schools and colleges — to assume that 100 percent of their students are using ChatGPT and other generative A.I. tools on every assignment, in every subject, unless they’re being physically supervised inside a school building

Second, schools should stop relying on A.I. detector programs to catch students cheating (sounds like an ill-conceived adversarial game)

My third piece of advice — and the one that may get me the most angry emails from teachers — is that teachers should focus less on warning students about the shortcomings of generative A.I. than figuring out what the technology does well.

Treat this year — the first full academic year of the post-ChatGPT era — as a learning experience, and don’t expect to get everything right.

  1. Dimentionality reduction (or the Univariable model that fits better than all Multivariable models)

  • driving force behind the most important algorithms in machine learning and data science: PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, autoencoders, and more

  • but also a projection of the creators’ human minds and thus the foundation of all human learning and intelligence (e.g., language, vision, motor control, etc.)

  • if all of the above is true, then dimensionality reduction is the most important concept in all of data science and machine learning (and perhaps all of science)

  • since life is a series and challenges both big and small, it quickly becomes apparent that dimensionality reduction is the most important concept in all of life. life becomes chaotic and unmanageable if all events are viewed as independent

  • a most celebrated skill or tool in life is the ability to see the big picture and connect the dots. this is the essence of dimensionality reduction. why else is the iphone such a success? it reduces the dimensionality of life by connecting the dots of communication, entertainment, and productivity. and why is chatGPT the most revolutionary tool in the history of education? it reduces the dimensionality of learning by connecting the dots of knowledge, creativity, and communication. all can now be found in one place and in one tool (a single dimension).

  1. Reducing the number of random variables under consideration by obtaining a set of principal variables (i.e., principal components) that account for most of the variance in the original data set (i.e., the data with a high number of dimensions)

  • when multivariable regressions are fit to data, the “fit” of the model to the data might be celebrated

  • but almost immediately concerns about overfitting arise and whether this model will generalize to new data

  • soon parsimony becomes a virtue and the model is reduced to a few variables. this is dimensionality reduction

  • therefore all didacting tools in life are dimensionality reduction tools. they reduce the dimensionality of life by connecting the dots of knowledge, creativity, and communication. all can now be found in one place and in one tool (a single dimension).

  • monotheistic religions of the world are generations on generations of dimentionality reduction down to a single dimension (e.g., one god, one book, one prophet, one savior, one path, one truth, one life, one way, one love, one ethos). the greeks of antiquity remain a special caze with their unseemingly infinite gods and goddesses. but even they had a pantheon of gods and goddesses that were reduced to a single dimension (e.g., zeus, hera, athena, apollo, etc.). and in that pantheon a hierarchy – like all hierarchies – with zeus at the top comes to the same thing as monotheistic religions.

  1. Education can thus be seen as providing a mechanism by which students recognize peers who are more capable than themselves, and then imitate them. Or approaches to challenges that utilize the fewest number of dimensions (i.e., the most parsimonious approach) are the most successful and emulate them

  • chatGPT seems to be that thing. Unlike a Google search query that returns a list of ranked links, chatGPT returns a single answer to offer the most parsimonious approach to learning.

  • this brings with it as much danger as a religion with zealots who have a single answer to all questions and never question the answer.

  • it is in this spirit that educators should embrace chatGPT and teach students to question the answers and answers to the questions.

  • by using AI to manage actual projects who success speaks for itself, educators can teach students to question the answers and answers to the questions.

  • As a medical doctor who woke up one day and started writing code, I can attest to the power of this approach. AI and Teaching and Learning in the 21st Century

Introduction:

  • Brief introduction of how AI has changed industries including the education sector.

  • Why it is crucial to adapt the education system to AI’s capabilities and pitfalls.

The Background and Inspiration

Our Father and His Challenges in Writing a Book:

  • A personal narrative of how someone close struggled with adapting to the modern way of doing things, emphasizing the difference in generations and technology adaptability.

The Need for Education to Adapt to AI:

  • Discuss the rapid pace of technological advancements.

  • The transition from traditional methods to technology-assisted teaching and learning.

  • The potential gaps and shortcomings if we fail to adapt.

Practical Processes for Getting Started on Using AI

  • Steps for integrating AI into the classroom:

    1. Understand the technology: What is AI? How does it function?

    2. Identify the needs: How can AI assist in the current education model?

    3. Choosing the right tools: E.g., Chatbots, tailored programs, AI-driven content recommendations.

    4. Continuous training and adaptation: Ensuring educators are up-to-date.

    5. Feedback loops: Ensuring a system of feedback to identify errors and areas for improvement.

Examples of a Curriculum with AI Integrated (for ages 8-15)

Introducing AI concepts:

  • Basic understanding of computers and algorithms.

  • Hands-on projects: Building simple chatbots or voice assistants.

  • Data and privacy: Ensuring children understand the implications of data sharing and AI ethics.

Advanced Curriculum:

  • Delving deeper into machine learning models.

  • Potential pitfalls and biases in AI.

  • Real-world applications: Use-cases and potential future developments.

Adaptations of AI to Teaching and Learning

Teacher Scattergram:

  • Using AI to assess teacher performances and adapt strategies.

  • Personalized feedback to educators for optimal outcomes.

Tailor-made Questions:

  • Adaptive learning: How AI can curate questions based on students’ progress and areas of struggle.

  • Continuous assessment: Ensuring students are on track and receiving support where needed.

In Conclusion:

The integration of AI in the education sector can prove to be revolutionary, but with great power comes great responsibility. The aim should be to harness the benefits while also cultivating a mindset that promotes continuous learning, questioning, and adaptability in the face of ever-evolving technological advancements.

Note: Ensure to maintain a balanced view. While AI has immense potential, over-reliance or blind trust can be detrimental. Encouraging critical thinking, even in an AI-assisted environment, remains paramount.
