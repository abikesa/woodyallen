Notes from Interim Dean and CEO Theodore DeWeese’s Townhall#

ELSI/ELSA in AI, Genomics, and Healthcare#

General Overview - Kadija Ferryman#

  • Ethical, Legal, and Social Aspects (ELSA) in Europe and Ethical, Legal, and Social Implications (ELSI) in the U.S.

  • Notion of ELSI conceived during Human Genome Project

  • Topics vary with AI’s maturity and the industry in question.

  • Answers will vary with point in lifetime of AI

  • This wil also vary with industry

Key Speakers from Hopkins#

  1. Kadija Ferryman: Anthropology & AI in healthcare, Guardrails in AI AI bias as a justice issue: algorithmic bias as a socio-tech issue (humans involved in process)

    • data are imprinted with human biases

    • a tool may perpetrate and exacerbate biases seen in humans

    • so no such thing as pure “objective” AI

    • privacy and security issues: AI works on a shit load of trained data

    • introduces so many risks

    • privacy and equity issues can have overlap (non-equal distribution of resources to “keep data private”)

    • bias-justice-privacy

  2. Suchi Saria: Standford, Machine learning, Bayesian health, EMR+AI AI in 2023

  • Small groups very focused on specific problems can beat big tech (e.g. Suchi Saria)

  • practical AI (deeper learning around 2012: computer imaging, speech recognition, natural language processing, etc.)

    • its an adult

  • generative AI (2014: GANs, creating new data similar to training data)

    • rich output

    • but needs rich output

    • very unreliable

    • its a fourth greater

    • needs some adult supervision (tinglong dai)

Difference between AI and autonomy?

  • AI is a tool, system, or process

  • When a system can make decisions on its own: autonomy

  • So AI is a tool to achieve autonomy

  • Gap: is application within exciting areas

  • Driving is a good example thats reaching maturity

  • The other concept is “augmentation”: complementary strength to humans

    • look at tons of human records without fatigue

    • think of data 24/7; no need to sleep

    • can process unstructured data (not just numerical data)

    • build workflows that are more efficient; less prone to errors; more consistent

    • superhero capes: harder, faster, stronger, better, etc. for professionals

  1. No chance that we can compete with big tech and lead the AI revolution

  2. Our role in AI in healthcare gives us a super-specific edge

  3. Bringing care closer to where patient lives

  4. Elliot Fishman: Hopkins, Radiology, Pancreatic cancer, early detection using AI

  • Worked with Steve Jobs/Hopkins - radiology (Been at Hopkins for 40 years)

  • Pancreatic cancer: 3rd leading cause of cancer death in the US

  • But if you pickup tumors early, survival reaches 70%

  • Largest % present beyond this

  • Surgery, oncology, gi, radiology, and microsoct ai team at hopkins: early detection

  • Accuracy in determining what cystic lesions are cancerous

  • Moved on to other diagnoses (ICD-10 codes)

    1. agent (rater)

    2. verb (diagnosis)

    3. object (patients)

    • 1.5 million patients

Worry about lossing jobs to AI?

  • For physicians who do not use AI, you’ll be replaced; since its a supplement to your work

  • Average clinicians can be made into super clinicians; so Hopkins in a small town, small country, etc.

  • Program can now look at 1000s of images with accuracy

More interactivity in education (think chatGPT)

  • no medical student wants a lecture (none can listen for >45min)

  • memorizing facts (AI can do a lot better)

  • so we must rework this

  1. Tinglong Dai: Carnegie, Business aspects, AI in healthcare

AI Landscape in 2023#

Types of AI - Tinglong Dai#

  • Practical AI: Matured around 2012 (e.g., computer imaging, NLP)

  • Generative AI: Emerged around 2014 (e.g., GANs)

Difference between AI and Autonomy - Tinglong Dai/Github Copilot#

  • AI: Tool, system, or process

  • Autonomy: System can make independent decisions

  • Gap: Application within exciting areas like driving

Augmentation vs. Replacement - Tinglong Dai#

  • AI can augment human capabilities.

  • Risk of job loss for professionals not adopting AI.

Concerns and Challenges - Kadija Ferryman#

  • AI Bias: Justice issue, human biases can get imprinted

  • Privacy and Security Risks: AI needs vast data to learn and these data can be sensitive

  • Interactivity in Education: Need to reconsider traditional methods

Other Roles of AI at JHU#

  • Finance

  • Human Resources

  • Personal Lives

  • Admissions

  • Tailored Courses

  • Robotics beyond healthcare

  • Predicting “No Shows”

Positioning for the Future#

  1. Can’t compete with Big Tech but have a niche in healthcare.

  2. Focus should be on specific problems.

  3. Importance of addressing bias, justice, and privacy.
