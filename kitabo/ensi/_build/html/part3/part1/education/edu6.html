Title

Redefining Educational Landscapes: The Impact of ChatGPT-3 on Skill, Challenge, and Flow.

Abstract

Traditional teaching methods have often been limited by the human constraints of the teacher. Advances in artificial intelligence, exemplified by ChatGPT-3, offer the possibility of overcoming these limitations. This transformation allows a shift in educational focus towards cultivating creativity, innovation, and entrepreneurship. By aligning challenges and skills more effectively, AI can change the dynamics of education and life

Whether addressing the challenges of life, work, school, family, community, or planet, the process is the same. Namely, identifying the challenges and rallying the community towards them, deploying of the most skilled to match the attendant challenges, and utilizing of tools that exponentiate the efficacy and safety of our solutions.

Matching our communal challenges with equal measures of skills and tools is the essence of not only education but life. This is the essence that is being transformed by the advent of AI as is represented by the yellow flow channel in the figure above.

In traditional educational settings, represented by the red flow channel in the graph above, anxiety often results from a mismatch between high-level challenges and low-level skills or tools available to meet them.

By contrast, the green flow channel in the figure above represents the idealized state of affairs where we perceive our challenges as substantively less than the skills and tools we have to address them. The Psalmist who wrote from the perspective of a lamb in the care of a shepherd understood this idealized state of affairs when he wrote, “he lays me down in green pastures, he leads me beside still waters, he restores my soul (from the red anxiety zone).”

Lets replace the shepherd in this metaphor with the teacher, whose rod and staff - AI - do comfort the student. And lets go further and replace the idealized green with yellow. In other words, lets imagine a world where the teacher recognizes the ever-evolving real-world challenges and constantly engages their students into never-ending creativity, innovation, and entrepreneurship. This is the essence of flow and the essence of education after chatGPT-3.

Skills

So what, then, are the skills educators need to develop in order to be effective in this new world? The answer is simple: the same skills that we want our students to develop. Namely, the ability to collaborate, interact, and persuasively communicate about projects that apply knowledge to solve real-world challenges. In summary: creativity, innovation, and entrepreneurship. Other steakholders in the education process, such as parents, administrators, and policy makers, need to develop these same skills as well.

Does this mean disruption, loss of control, and chaos? Yes, but only in the short term. In the long term, it means a more effective and efficient education system that is more responsive to the needs of the students and the community. It means more engaged and fulfilled students, more effective and efficient teachers, and less patronizing and controlling administrators.

Education curriculums are typically rigid and assume that all students are the same. But students begin at different points and evolve uniquely over time, yielding adults who are as different as the stars in the sky - which is a good thing.

Concerns about morality and ethics in AI-fueled pedagogy are expected, since morality and ethics emphasize uniformity. But the same concerns were raised about the printing press, the automobile, and the internet. The solution is the same as well: adoption of the new tools and freeing up time to focus on the things that humans are uniquely good at.

Those who are uncomfortable about the maturity of children and their ability to handle the freedom that comes with this new world should remember that children are already exposed to the internet and AI. The question is not whether they will be exposed to it, but whether they will leverage it to their advantage or not in the context of education.

Tools

From the beginning of time until now, the tools of learning have been oral tradition, didactic instruction, books, classrooms, and examination. But of what use is committing facts to memory when they are readily available on the internet? And in a post chatGPT-3 world, of what use is the book when the student can ask the AI to summarize it for them?

A question not to ask: of what use is didactic instruction when the teacher is not the most knowledgeable person in the room? Of what use is the classroom when the students are not physically present? Of what use is examination when the students are not being taught to apply knowledge to solve real-world challenges?

When we recognize that the problem of education is the same as the problem of life, work, family, community, and planet, we can begin to see that the tools of education are the same as the tools of life. Namely, the ability to identify the challenges we face, the ability to match them with the skills we have, and the ability to utilize the tools we have to address them. And being afraid of tools is not a solution.

Verdict

The verdict is in: the tools of education are the same as the tools of life. Namely, the ability to identify the challenges we face, the ability to match them with the skills we have, and the ability to utilize the tools we have to address them. And being afraid of tools is not a solution. Let’s direct our energy towards aligning children with their challenges, skills, and tools, and let’s not be afraid of the AI that will help us do that. We unreservedly believe this is an inflection point in the history of education and we are excited to be a part of it.
